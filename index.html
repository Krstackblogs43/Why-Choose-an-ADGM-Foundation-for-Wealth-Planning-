What is an ADGM Foundation?

An ADGM Foundation is a legal entity established under Abu Dhabi Global Market regulations, specifically designed to hold and manage assets without shareholders. It offers significant advantages for asset protection, succession planning, and operational flexibility, making it an ideal solution for private individuals, families, or corporations. 

With a separate legal personality, governance defined by its charter and by-laws, and strong privacy protections, an ADGM foundation can pursue private, charitable, or commercial objectives while safeguarding wealth. Increasingly, high-net-worth families, family offices, and entrepreneurs are leveraging ADGM foundations to secure their assets and plan for the future, integrating them into comprehensive wealth protection strategies. ADGM Foundation Setup: Step-by-Step

Setting up a foundation in ADGM involves the following stages:

  1. Draft the Charter and By-Laws
    • Define the foundation’s objectives, beneficiaries, and rules for management.
    • Include succession plans and operational protocols.
  2. Appoint the Founder and Council
    • The founder establishes the foundation.
    • The council (board of managers) oversees operations and compliance.
  3. Register with ADGM
    • Submit documents to the ADGM Registration Authority.
    • Include identity verification for founders and council members.
  4. Asset Transfer
    • Initial endowment can include cash, real estate, investments, or shares.
  5. Compliance and Reporting
    • Maintain records and meet AML/CFT regulations.
    • Annual reporting may be required for commercial activities.

Governance Structure of ADGM Foundations

Effective governance is critical for an ADGM foundation to ensure it operates according to the founder’s intentions while maintaining full regulatory compliance. The foundation’s governance framework typically involves several key roles. The founder is responsible for setting the foundation’s objectives, appointing council members, and defining succession rules to safeguard long-term continuity. Council members oversee the day-to-day management of the foundation, including asset administration, compliance with ADGM regulations, and execution of the charter’s provisions. 

Beneficiaries are entitled to receive benefits as defined in the foundation’s charter and by-laws, ensuring their interests are protected and clearly outlined. An optional protector can also be appointed to monitor council decisions, providing an additional layer of oversight to ensure the founder’s objectives are faithfully executed.

Best practices in foundation governance include holding regular council meetings with documented minutes, maintaining transparent reporting to beneficiaries, and conducting annual audits to verify financial integrity and operational accountability. With expert guidance, these practices help minimize the risk of disputes, maintain compliance, and ensure the foundation achieves its long-term objectives.

Compliance Requirements

ADGM foundations are subject to strict regulatory requirements to ensure proper governance and compliance. Foundations must be registered with the ADGM Registration Authority, and if they engage in commercial activities, financial reporting is required. Additionally, all foundations must comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CFT) regulations. 

Proper record-keeping is essential, including documenting council meetings, financial transactions, and distributions to beneficiaries. Non-compliance can result in fines or suspension, making expert guidance essential to ensure regulatory adherence while maintaining confidentiality and protecting the foundation’s integrity.

FAQ – ADGM Foundations

Q1: What is the difference between an ADGM foundation and a trust?
A: Foundations have legal personality, can own assets directly, and are managed by a council rather than trustees.

Q2: Can foreigners establish an ADGM foundation?
A: Yes, non-UAE residents can form foundations in ADGM and appoint council members internationally.

Q3: How long does registration take?
A: Typically 2–4 weeks depending on documentation and council appointments.

Q4: Are ADGM foundations subject to UAE taxes?
A: Foundations benefit from the UAE’s favorable tax regime, but commercial activities may require corporate tax compliance.

Q5: Can foundations hold real estate?
A: Yes, foundations can own property across the UAE.

Q6: Is there a minimum endowment?
A: No strict requirement, though sufficient funding is recommended for the foundation to achieve its objectives.
